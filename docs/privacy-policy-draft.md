# Draft privacy and AI use note for StudyTrack

This draft is a starting point only and must be reviewed by the school, sector, and legal/privacy contacts before deployment.

## Product purpose
StudyTrack is a student productivity tool designed to help students record homework, organise study time, track progress, and store evidence of completed work.

## Local-first design
The prototype is designed to store student planner data on the student's own device first. This may include:
- subjects
- homework tasks
- study sessions
- goals
- reflections
- evidence records

## Data minimisation
The app should collect only the information needed to provide the service. Sensitive information should not be collected unless the school has a clear lawful basis, a defined purpose, and an approved governance process.

## Teacher-published content
Teachers may publish homework items, revision checklists, assessment reminders, and study resources to a class or subject stream. These posts should be auditable and clearly labelled with teacher name, class, and publish date.

## AI boundaries
Any AI feature should be limited to:
- checklist generation
- study plan suggestions
- simplification of instructions
- self-testing questions from teacher-provided material
- reflection prompts

AI should not be used to generate final assessable responses for students or to bypass teacher directions.

## Transparency
Schools should provide a clear notice that explains:
- what data is stored
- where it is stored
- who can access it
- whether any information leaves the device
- whether AI tools are used
- how students can export or delete their data

## Governance controls
Recommended controls include:
- role-based access
- retention settings
- deletion/export tools
- optional local-only mode
- optional AI-off mode
- audit trail for teacher-published items
